# References and Further Reading

## Academic References

### Foundational Robotics Textbooks

1. **Siciliano, B., & Khatib, O. (Eds.). (2016).** *Springer Handbook of Robotics (2nd ed.)*. Springer.
   - Comprehensive reference covering all aspects of robotics research and applications
   - Essential for understanding the breadth of robotics as a field

2. **Spong, M. W., Hutchinson, S., & Vidyasagar, M. (2006).** *Robot Modeling and Control*. John Wiley & Sons.
   - Fundamental text on robot kinematics, dynamics, and control
   - Mathematical foundation for understanding robotic systems

3. **Thrun, S., Burgard, W., & Fox, D. (2005).** *Probabilistic Robotics*. MIT Press.
   - Definitive guide to probabilistic approaches in robotics
   - Essential for understanding uncertainty in robot perception and control

4. **Craig, J. J. (2005).** *Introduction to Robotics: Mechanics and Control (3rd ed.)*. Pearson.
   - Classic textbook on robot mechanics and control
   - Excellent for understanding manipulator kinematics and dynamics

5. **Lynch, K. M., & Park, F. C. (2017).** *Modern Robotics: Mechanics, Planning, and Control*. Cambridge University Press.
   - Modern approach to robotics using screw theory and geometric methods
   - Includes online resources and MATLAB/Python code

### Specialized Robotics Literature

6. **LaValle, S. M. (2006).** *Planning Algorithms*. Cambridge University Press.
   - Comprehensive coverage of motion planning algorithms
   - Available online at http://planning.cs.uiuc.edu/

7. **Murphy, R. R. (2019).** *Introduction to AI Robotics (2nd ed.)*. MIT Press.
   - Focus on AI techniques in robotics applications
   - Good bridge between AI and robotics

8. **Hartley, R., & Zisserman, A. (2003).** *Multiple View Geometry in Computer Vision (2nd ed.)*. Cambridge University Press.
   - Definitive text on computer vision geometry
   - Essential for visual robotics applications

9. **Quigley, M., et al. (2009).** "ROS: an open-source Robot Operating System." *ICRA Workshop on Open Source Software*.
   - Original paper introducing the Robot Operating System
   - Foundational for understanding ROS architecture

10. **Macenski, S., et al. (2022).** "ROS 2: Next Generation Robot Operating System." *arXiv preprint*.
    - Update on the second generation of ROS
    - Important for understanding modern robotics middleware

## Research Papers and Conference Proceedings

### Top Robotics Conferences

20. **IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA)**
    - Premier robotics research conference
    - Proceedings available through IEEE Xplore

21. **International Conference on Robotics: Science and Systems (RSS)**
    - High-quality peer-reviewed robotics research
    - Open access proceedings available online

22. **IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS)**
    - Major international robotics conference
    - Covers all aspects of robotics research

23. **International Journal of Robotics Research (IJRR)**
    - Leading peer-reviewed robotics journal
    - High-impact research papers

24. **IEEE Transactions on Robotics**
    - Top-tier journal for robotics research
    - Mathematical and theoretical foundations

### Seminal Papers

25. **Montemerlo, M., et al. (2002).** "FastSLAM: A factored solution to the simultaneous localization and mapping problem." *AAAI*.
    - Groundbreaking paper on SLAM using particle filters
    - Fundamental to modern localization approaches

26. **LaValle, S. M., & Kuffner, J. J. (2001).** "Randomized kinodynamic planning." *International Journal of Robotics Research*.
    - Introduced RRT algorithm for motion planning
    - Influential for sampling-based planning

27. **Fox, D., Burgard, W., & Thrun, S. (1997).** "The dynamic window approach to collision avoidance." *IEEE Robotics & Automation Magazine*.
    - Introduced DWA for local navigation
    - Widely used in mobile robotics

28. **Dudek, G., & Jenkin, M. (2010).** *Computational Principles of Mobile Robotics (2nd ed.)*. Cambridge University Press.
    - Comprehensive treatment of mobile robotics algorithms
    - Good mathematical foundation
